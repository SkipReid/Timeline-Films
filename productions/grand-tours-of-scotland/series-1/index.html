Grand Tours of Scotland - Series 1

With a Victorian guidebook in his hands, historian Paul Murton travels the country and traces the changes that have taken place since the birth of Scottish tourism 200 years ago.

About the show

For centuries, "north of the border" had been regarded as a place to avoid and early travellers complained about the savage terrain and uncouth habits of the natives. To find out what changed to make Scotland an internationally celebrated tourist destination Paul recreates six journeys suggested by a well thumbed copy of "Black’s Picturesque Guide to Scotland" and follows in the footsteps of the first tourists to come here.

  • The Romantic Ideal - ep1

    First broadcast: BBC Scotland - October 6th 2010.
    Other Broadcasts: BBC HD - 18th May 2011 @ 7pm

    In this first episode, historian Paul Murton goes in search of "The Romantic ideal" and discovers how the famous writer Sir Walter Scott played a key role in re-inventing Scotland, convincing his readers that its stark landscapes were "sublime" rather than foreboding, the natives noble not savage, and the weather romantic not dreary.

    Travelling from the Trossachs, out to Iona and then the fabled Isle of Staffa Paul visits the iconic destinations that enchanted and inspired writers, artists and early tourists with the magic of Scotland’s unique history and landscape.

  • The Sporting Life - ep2

    First broadcast: BBC Scotland - October 13th 2010.
    Other Broadcasts: BBC HD - 18th May 2011 @ 7pm

    In the spirit of Victorian manliness, historian Paul Murton continues his journey on an original 1870’s Tricycle. Enjoying the dubious delights of this unusual mode of transport, Paul travels north from Dunkeld, through the mountains to royal Deeside to discover how Scotland’s mountains and glens became a playground for rich gentlemen, eager to test themselves against the forces of nature.

  • In Search of the real Scotland - ep3

    First broadcast: BBC Scotland - October 20th 2010.
    Other Broadcasts: BBC HD - 18th May 2011 @ 7pm

    For centuries, outsiders had seldom visited the beautiful landscape of the west coast of Scotland, but then the power of steam changed everything. Within a century, a network of railways spread across the country, connecting the industrial cities of the south to the mountains and glens of the north. And with the trains came the tourists - all clamouring for a piece of the real Scotland. In this episode, historian Paul Murton takes the beautiful Jacobite steam train to Mallaig and then onto the magical Isle of Skye.

  • Mind Body and Spirit - ep4

    First broadcast: BBC Scotland - November 3rd 2010.
    Other Broadcasts: BBC HD - 18th May 2011 @ 7pm

    Historian Paul Murton goes in search of the stunning landscape of the highlands that has attracted visitors for the last two hundred years with the promise of improving "mind, body and spirit".

    Paul traces the history of the great outdoors, and discovers how our mountains, lochs and glens were increasingly seen as a giant playground, where people of all classes could escape the dull routine of the modern world. His journey takes him from the shores of Loch Tay, across the great wilderness of Rannoch Moor, and sees him climbs the iconic mountain of Buachaille Etive Mor.

  • Perfect Isolation - ep5

    First broadcast: BBC Scotland - Novembber 10th 2010.
    Other Broadcasts: BBC HD - 18th May 2011 @ 7pm

    Keen to escape the noise and pressures of their overcrowded world, more adventurous tourists braved the rough seas to travel to Orkney and Shetland, hoping to restore themselves in the peace and quiet of the far north.

    Historian Paul Murton travels to the Northern Isles to discover how their remoteness from the mainland became a draw for tourists "in search of perfect isolation". Paul’s journey begins on board a Norwegian racing yacht in the ocean to the east of Shetland. Landing at Lerwick, he continues to explore the main island and its fabulous wildlife before heading out to sea again and sailing south to the musical Orkney islands.

  • Wish You Were Here - ep6

    First broadcast: BBC Scotland - Novembber 17th 2010.
    Other Broadcasts: BBC HD - 18th May 2011 @ 7pm

    In Victorian times, most tourists came from a tiny social elite - the rich - for the great mass of the population a holiday was just a dream. But when working people eventually won the right to some free time, by far the majority of them didn’t rush off to the romantic highlands to enjoy the view, most postcards home were sent from the seaside. In the final episode of "Grand Tours of Scotland," historian Paul Murton traces the rise of the seaside as a workers’ playground.
