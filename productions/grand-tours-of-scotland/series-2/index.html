Grand Tours of Scotland - Series 2

In the Victorian era, tourists flocked to Scotland to explore its unique blend of stunning scenery, romantic ruins, myths and legends. Paul Murton returns with a second series of "Grand Tours of Scotland" to visit the most fascinating parts of the country that have charmed travellers for more than 200 years.

Crossing the Border

Episode 1

In the 1800’s, Scotland was regarded by English tourists as an exotic destination. Making the short journey over the Union Bridge at Berwick was to pass from one world into another: from England, where they felt secure in known routines, to Scotland: a very different world of novelty and adventure. To find out about the allure of Scotland for early tourists, Paul boards a traditional gypsy caravan to travel through Kelso, Melrose, New Lanark and up to Glasgow.

Elemental Beauty

Episode 2

In this episode, Paul is braving the weather and making his journey on foot in order to experience nature in all its elemental glory - which sometimes means four seasons in one day !

Paul begins his journey on beautiful Loch Maree, where he visits the unique "island within an island" of Eilean Subhain, before venturing into the wilds of Assynt and Cape Wrath, ending his odyssey at the northern most part of mainland Scotland, Duncansby Head.

Islands of the Clyde

Episode 3

For generations, the Firth of Clyde was a favourite holiday destination for millions of Scots, both rich and poor. Here you could enjoy healthy sea breezes - take a dunk in the briny, mess about in boats, enjoy a glass or two of your favourite tipple - or, if you had the money - all of the above at the same time ! Paul begins his island hopping escapade at Rothesay on Bute before heading over to Cumbrae and finally the iconic island of Ailsa Craig.

The Charms of Nature

Episode 4

Paul travels by vintage bike through the spectacular scenery of the central highlands to appreciate the charms of nature. His two wheeled adventure on a classic 1920’s Humber bike starts in Glen Lyon, and takes in the beautiful village of Fortinghall, home to the oldest tree in Scotland - believed to be an incredible 2,000 years old. Paul’s cycle tour takes him onto the popular tourist destinations of Pitlochry and Newtonmore, before ending in the stunning Cairngorms National Park, where Ospreys, are once again thriving.

The True Spirit of Scotland

Episode 5

Following a network of now forgotten steamer routes, Paul goes in search of the true spirit of Scotland. Beginning at Loch Eck, Paul travels from loch to loch in Argyll, stopping off to visit Inverary and the ancient site of Dunadd at Kilmartin Glen before travelling across to Islay. As the steamers left the Scottish lochs years ago, Paul has to literally "paddle his own canoe" to follow the route - in this case, a beautiful replica of an original Rob Roy canoe from the 1890’s.

In Search of Sunshine

Episode 6

Long before the era of cheap flights and package holidays on the "Costa del Sunburn," most Scots spent their summer holidays by the Scottish seaside. In the last episode of Series II, Paul goes in search of sunshine on the east coast which claims to have the best record for sunlight in Scotland. To capture the holiday spirit and to chase the sun, Paul travels by Morris Minor to follow his route up the east coast from Carnoustie to Montrose, Stonehaven, and on to Aberdeen.
